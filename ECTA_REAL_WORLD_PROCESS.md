# ECTA Real-World Coffee Export Process - Ethiopia

## Based on Actual ECTA Regulations and Procedures

This document outlines the **actual** coffee export process in Ethiopia as governed by the Ethiopian Coffee & Tea Authority (ECTA), based on current regulations including Directive 1106/2025.

---

## ECTA's Role and Mandate

The Ethiopian Coffee and Tea Authority (ECTA) is the primary regulatory body responsible for:
- Issuing export licenses and permits
- Quality control and certification
- Coffee grading and classification
- Export authorization
- Laboratory certification
- Competence certificate management
- Market oversight and compliance

---

## Pre-Export Requirements (Before Any Export Activity)

### 1. Business Registration and Capital Requirements

**Directive 1106/2025 Requirements:**

| Exporter Type | Minimum Capital Required |
|---------------|-------------------------|
| Private Coffee Exporters | ETB 15 million (previously ETB 1 million) |
| Trade Associations | ETB 20 million (previously ETB 1.5 million) |
| Joint Stock Companies | ETB 20 million |
| Limited Liability Companies | ETB 20 million |
| Farmer-Exporters | Exempt from capital requirements |

**Required Documents:**
- Business registration certificate
- Tax Identification Number (TIN)
- Proof of minimum capital (bank statement)
- Company profile and ownership structure
- Physical office address

### 2. ECTA-Certified Coffee Laboratory (Mandatory)

**New Requirement under Directive 1106/2025:**

All exporters (except farmer-exporters) must:
- Operate an **ECTA-certified coffee laboratory**
- Employ a **qualified coffee taster** with:
  - Minimum diploma qualification
  - Valid and renewed proficiency certificate
  - Exclusive employment (one taster per coffee dispatcher)

**Laboratory Requirements:**
- Physical laboratory space
- Required equipment for coffee testing
- Quality control procedures
- ECTA inspection and certification
- Annual renewal

### 3. Exporter's Competence Certificate

**Purpose:** Demonstrates exporter's capability to handle coffee exports

**Requirements:**
- Valid business registration
- Minimum capital proof
- ECTA-certified laboratory
- Qualified taster on staff
- Storage facility inspection
- Quality management system

**Process:**
1. Submit application to ECTA
2. ECTA inspects facilities and laboratory
3. Verify taster qualifications
4. Issue competence certificate (if approved)
5. Annual renewal required

**Note:** Directive 1106/2025 aims to prevent misuse of competence certificates

### 4. Export License from ECTA

**Purpose:** Authorization to engage in coffee export activities

**Requirements:**
- Valid competence certificate
- Exporter's Registration Certificate from Ethiopian Investment Commission (EIC)
- Proof of minimum capital
- Clean business record
- Compliance with previous export obligations (if any)

**Process:**
1. Submit application with required documents
2. ECTA reviews business credentials
3. Verify competence certificate validity
4. Issue export license
5. License valid for specific period (typically annual)

## Coffee Sourcing and Procurement

### 5. Coffee Purchase from ECX or Direct Sources

**Options:**
- **ECX (Ethiopian Commodity Exchange):** Purchase verified coffee lots
- **Direct from Farmers/Cooperatives:** For exporters with direct sourcing agreements
- **Farmer-Exporters:** Own production

**ECX Process:**
1. Register as ECX member
2. Purchase coffee lot through ECX trading
3. Receive ECX lot number
4. Receive warehouse receipt number
5. Coffee stored in ECX-certified warehouse

**Required Information:**
- ECX lot number
- Warehouse receipt number
- Warehouse location
- Coffee type and origin
- Quantity (in kg)
- Grade (preliminary)

---

## ECTA Quality Inspection and Certification

### 6. Pre-Export Quality Inspection (Mandatory)

**Conducted by:** Ethiopian Coffee Quality and Control Authority (under ECTA)

**Process:**
1. Exporter requests quality inspection
2. Submit coffee samples to ECTA inspection center
3. ECTA conducts physical inspection:
   - Bean size analysis
   - Moisture content testing
   - Defect analysis
   - Foreign matter detection
4. **Cupping evaluation** by certified tasters:
   - Flavor profile
   - Aroma assessment
   - Acidity level
   - Body characteristics
   - Overall quality score

**Inspection Centers:**
- Addis Ababa (main center)
- Dire Dawa
- Regional centers (expanding)

**Output:**
- Quality Certificate
- Coffee Grade (Grade 1-9, with Grade 1 being highest)
- Cupping score
- Defect count
- Moisture percentage

**Quality Standards:**
- Specialty Coffee: Grade 1-2
- Premium Coffee: Grade 3-4
- Standard Export: Grade 5-7
- Below Standard: Grade 8-9 (limited export)

### 7. Coffee Grading and Classification

**Grading Criteria:**
- Physical characteristics (bean size, uniformity)
- Defect count (primary and secondary defects)
- Moisture content (optimal: 11-12%)
- Cupping quality (flavor, aroma, body, acidity)
- Processing method (washed, natural, honey)

**Classification by Origin:**
- Sidamo (Sidama)
- Yirgacheffe
- Harrar (Harar)
- Limu
- Jimma
- Lekempti
- Bebeka
- Tepi

## Export Documentation and Authorization

### 8. Export Permit Application

**Required Documents:**
1. Valid export license from ECTA
2. Valid competence certificate
3. Quality certificate (from Step 6)
4. ECX lot number and warehouse receipt
5. Sales contract with buyer
6. Proforma invoice
7. Packing list

**Process:**
1. Submit complete documentation to ECTA
2. ECTA verifies all documents
3. ECTA issues export permit for specific consignment
4. Permit valid for single shipment

### 9. Certificate of Origin

**Issued by:** ECTA

**Purpose:** Certifies Ethiopian origin of coffee

**Requirements:**
- Valid export permit
- Quality certificate
- Sales contract
- Buyer information

**Information Included:**
- Exporter details
- Buyer/importer details
- Coffee type and origin region
- Quantity
- Grade
- Destination country
- Shipment details

### 10. Sample Export Authorization

**For sending coffee samples to potential buyers:**

**Process:**
1. Submit service request to ECTA
2. Provide exporter competence assurance
3. Submit application letter
4. Receive tracking number
5. ECTA reviews and approves
6. Issue sample export authorization
7. Track application progress

**Limitations:**
- One application per request
- Reapplication only after completion
- May require physical appointment for payment/pickup

## Banking and Foreign Exchange

### 11. Export Registration with Commercial Bank

**Process:**
1. Register export order with commercial bank
2. Bank issues export permit for consignment
3. Bank handles foreign exchange allocation
4. Bank coordinates with National Bank of Ethiopia (NBE)

**Note:** Coffee exports are registered with NBE, not commercial banks (special regulation)

### 12. Foreign Exchange Application

**Handled by:** Commercial Bank on behalf of exporter

**Requirements:**
- All ECTA documentation
- Sales contract
- Proforma invoice
- Letter of credit (if applicable)

**NBE Review:**
- Verifies export value
- Approves FX allocation
- Monitors repatriation requirements

---

## Customs and Shipping

### 13. Customs Declaration

**Required Documents:**
- Export permit from ECTA
- Quality certificate
- Certificate of origin
- Commercial invoice
- Packing list
- Bill of lading (from shipping line)
- Customs declaration form
- Phytosanitary certificate

**Process:**
1. Submit documents to customs
2. Customs inspection (if required)
3. Customs clearance
4. Release for shipment

### 14. Packaging and Containerization

**Standard Packaging:**
- Jute or sisal bags
- 60-70 kg per bag
- Proper labeling (origin, grade, lot number)
- Moisture-proof lining
- Secure container loading

**Container Requirements:**
- Clean, dry containers
- Proper ventilation
- Protection from moisture and pests
- Temperature control (if required)

### 15. Shipping and Logistics

**Main Export Routes:**
- Port of Djibouti (primary)
- Port of Berbera (Somalia)
- Air freight (for samples and specialty coffee)

**Shipping Documents:**
- Bill of Lading
- Commercial Invoice
- Packing List
- Certificate of Origin
- Quality Certificate
- Phytosanitary Certificate
- Insurance Certificate

---

## Post-Export Requirements

### 16. Payment Receipt and FX Repatriation

**Requirements:**
- Exporter must repatriate foreign exchange earnings
- Payment through official banking channels
- NBE monitors compliance
- Deadline for repatriation (typically 90-120 days)

### 17. Export Performance Reporting

**Exporters must:**
- Report export performance to ECTA
- Submit shipping documents
- Confirm payment receipt
- Maintain export records

**ECTA Monitoring:**
- Track export volumes
- Monitor quality compliance
- Verify payment repatriation
- Assess exporter performance

## Complete Real-World Export Process Flow

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ PHASE 1: PRE-QUALIFICATION (One-time/Annual)               │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
1. Business Registration + Minimum Capital (ETB 15-20M)
2. Establish ECTA-Certified Laboratory
3. Hire Qualified Taster (diploma + proficiency certificate)
4. Apply for Competence Certificate → ECTA inspects → Issues certificate
5. Apply for Export License → ECTA reviews → Issues license
6. Register with Ethiopian Investment Commission (EIC)

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ PHASE 2: COFFEE PROCUREMENT                                 │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
7. Purchase coffee from ECX or direct sources
8. Receive ECX lot number + warehouse receipt
9. Coffee stored in certified warehouse

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ PHASE 3: QUALITY CERTIFICATION (Per Shipment)              │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
10. Request ECTA quality inspection
11. Submit coffee samples to ECTA inspection center
12. ECTA conducts:
    - Physical inspection (size, moisture, defects)
    - Cupping evaluation (flavor, aroma, body, acidity)
13. ECTA issues Quality Certificate + Grade

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ PHASE 4: CONTRACT AND EXPORT AUTHORIZATION                 │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
14. Finalize sales contract with buyer
15. Prepare proforma invoice and packing list
16. Submit export permit application to ECTA with:
    - Export license
    - Competence certificate
    - Quality certificate
    - Sales contract
    - ECX documentation
17. ECTA reviews and issues Export Permit
18. ECTA issues Certificate of Origin

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ PHASE 5: BANKING AND FOREIGN EXCHANGE                      │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
19. Register export with Commercial Bank
20. Bank submits FX application to NBE
21. NBE approves FX allocation

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ PHASE 6: CUSTOMS AND SHIPPING                              │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
22. Package coffee (60-70kg jute bags)
23. Load into containers
24. Submit customs declaration with all documents
25. Customs inspection and clearance
26. Ship to destination port (Djibouti/Berbera)

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ PHASE 7: POST-EXPORT COMPLIANCE                            │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
27. Receive payment from buyer
28. Repatriate foreign exchange through bank
29. Report to ECTA and NBE
30. Maintain export records
```

## Key Differences from Current System Implementation

### ❌ Current System Issues:

1. **No pre-qualification check** - Exporters can create requests without licenses
2. **No competence certificate validation** - System doesn't verify exporter capability
3. **No laboratory requirement** - Missing ECTA-certified lab validation
4. **Wrong sequence** - Quality inspection happens after request creation
5. **Missing capital verification** - No check for minimum capital requirements
6. **No taster validation** - System doesn't verify qualified taster employment

### ✅ Required System Changes:

1. **Pre-Registration Module**
   - Exporter profile with business details
   - Capital verification
   - Laboratory certification tracking
   - Taster qualification management
   - Competence certificate issuance
   - Export license management

2. **Validation Gates**
   - Block export request creation without valid license
   - Require competence certificate before any export activity
   - Verify laboratory certification
   - Check taster qualifications
   - Validate minimum capital

3. **Quality Inspection Integration**
   - Pre-export quality inspection request
   - ECTA inspection scheduling
   - Cupping results recording
   - Grade assignment
   - Quality certificate issuance

4. **Export Authorization Workflow**
   - Sales contract registration
   - Export permit application
   - Certificate of origin issuance
   - Document verification

## Conclusion

The real-world ECTA process requires **extensive pre-qualification** before any export activity. The system must enforce:

1. ✅ Minimum capital requirements (ETB 15-20M)
2. ✅ ECTA-certified laboratory operation
3. ✅ Qualified taster employment
4. ✅ Valid competence certificate
5. ✅ Valid export license
6. ✅ Pre-export quality inspection
7. ✅ Sales contract registration
8. ✅ Export permit for each shipment
9. ✅ Certificate of origin

Only after ALL these requirements are met can an exporter create an export request in the blockchain system.
